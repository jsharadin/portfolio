# WHO Health Disparities Analysis

## Overview

A **digital-humanities + data-analysis** project investigating disparities in healthcare access and quality through the lens of socio-economic status, race, and gender. Using data from the **World Health Organization (WHO) Global Health Observatory** and complementary sources, the project explores how structural inequalities shape health outcomes — with a focus on marginalized communities.

Completed at UC Berkeley.

## Key Components

- **Annotated Bibliography** — curated research sources on health disparities.
- **Data Critique** — critical examination of the datasets used.
- **WHO Data Analysis** — statistical insights drawn from WHO's Global Health Observatory.
- **COVID Vaccine Data Analysis** — disparities in vaccination rates across populations.

## Intersectional Frameworks Applied

- **Critical Race Theory (CRT)** — examining structural racism in healthcare.
- **Critical Disability Theory (CDT)** — investigating accessibility barriers for disabled patients.
- **Feminist Theory** — exploring gender biases in healthcare policies and outcomes.

## Research Focus

- How socio-economic status affects access to preventive care.
- Racial disparities in healthcare quality and patient outcomes.
- Gender-based healthcare inequities and maternal health disparities.

## Tech Stack

- **Python** (pandas, NumPy, matplotlib, seaborn)
- **Jupyter Notebook**
- **WHO Global Health Observatory** open data
